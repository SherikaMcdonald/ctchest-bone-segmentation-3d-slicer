CT Chest Bone Segmentation and 3D Reconstruction Workflow Notes

Dataset:
CTChest sample dataset from 3D Slicer.

Objective:
The objective of this project was to create a clean segmentation of bone structures from a chest CT scan and generate a final 3D model suitable for portfolio presentation.

Workflow Summary:
1. Loaded the CTChest sample dataset in 3D Slicer.
2. Reviewed the original CT scan in axial, sagittal, coronal, and 3D views.
3. Used threshold-based selection to isolate high-density bone structures.
4. Reviewed the highlighted bone selection across multiple anatomical planes.
5. Removed small disconnected regions from the segmentation.
6. Applied smoothing to improve the final segmentation surface.
7. Generated a 3D model from the cleaned segmentation.
8. Exported the final model in STL, OBJ, and PLY formats.
9. Saved screenshots to document each major stage of the workflow.

Threshold and Cleanup Settings:
- Minimum bone threshold: 210
- Maximum bone threshold: 3000
- Small island removal size: 750
- Morphological closing kernel: 1.2 mm

Portfolio Purpose:
This project demonstrates medical image segmentation, anatomical visualization, 3D reconstruction, and AI training data preparation using 3D Slicer.
